## 1\. Data Science vs. Computer Science – Overview

Data science and computer science are two of the most popular domains in the technology sector. They are both in-demand fields with many career opportunities. However, choosing between the two of these fields can be a little confusing. Both of them require professionals to work with software applications and be familiar with the technology. 

Apart from these points, data science and computer science are vastly different. Computer science studies the theory and practice of how computers operate. A computer science professional learns about programming, operating systems, software, and all the knowledge related to running a computer. Their entire focus of the study is on computer systems. 

Data science is different. It is an interdisciplinary field that focuses on working with large amounts of structured and unstructured data. It uses computer science and statistics to perform statistical analysis. A data science professional uses multiple tools and programming languages such as Hadoop, Apache Spark, R, and Python. They also use calculus, deep learning, machine learning, and databases. 

Along with this distinction, data science is focused more on applied sciences. On the other hand, computer science focuses more on the theoretical aspects. Data science applies the principles and algorithms studied in computer science.

## 2\. Data Science vs. Computer Science – Job Opportunities

The fundamental concepts differ significantly in these domains. Data science roles focus heavily on leveraging machine learning and big data for finding insights, whereas computer science roles work on software and specific solutions. As a result, the job opportunities present in these fields also vary.

Both of these fields are laden with lucrative careers. Data science vs. computer science careers also differ in terms of their responsibilities, which we have discussed in the next section. 

![Data Science vs. Computer Science - Job Opportunities](https://www.analytixlabs.co.in/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/Blog-Image-1-2-1024x597.png)

### Career Opportunities in Data Science

Following are the prominent data science jobs: 

#### i) Data scientist

A data scientist is responsible for analyzing, processing, and modeling data. They interpret the insights generated from their analyses to create plans and help in the decision-making process. 

#### ii) Business intelligence developer

Business intelligence developers use data analytics and tools to find and share business-related information with the company’s stakeholders. They are responsible for creating and maintaining company business intelligence tools for which they assist in developing strategies. They have to share technical information with non-technical audiences so that they can understand them properly. 

#### iii) Data engineer

Data engineers prepare the Big data infrastructure that data scientists use. They are well-versed in using multiple big data technologies, including Hive, Pig, Hadoop, SQL, NoSQL, etc. Data engineers build systems that collect, store, and analyze data at scale.

### Career Opportunities In Computer Science

#### i) Computer scientist

A computer scientist writes and programs software to develop applications. They validate and develop models that can interact between people and computers or devices. Computer scientists study and test various algorithms and applications to understand their use and potential. 

#### ii) Software engineer

A software engineer is responsible for developing a specific component of a software application according to the specifications provided by the designers. They work in teams and are also responsible for updating the developed product to match its users’ requirements. 

#### iii) Web developer

Another typical role among computer science professionals is a web developer. A web developer writes the required code to create a website. They also take care of any technical issues that arise on the website.

### Data Science Responsibilities:

The primary responsibilities in a data science role are: 

#### Data mining

**_[Data mining](https://www.analytixlabs.co.in/blog/data-mining-techniques/)_** refers to extracting and identifying patterns in large data sets using statistics, machine learning, and database systems. As a data science professional, you’ll have to collect data from multiple sources that you choose according to their quality and reliability. You’ll also have to clean and optimize the data, so it generates accurate results. 

#### Analyzing data

One of the critical responsibilities of data science professionals is to analyze data for finding insights. They use various machine learning algorithms and deep learning implementations to see trends and patterns in the data. Some of the most common machine learning algorithms they use are classification, decision trees, random forest, etc. 

#### Sharing insights 

As a data science professional, you’ll have to share the insights you gathered from the data. Data science professionals usually share their insights with the stakeholders, such as the business leaders and investors. It requires them to be adept at storytelling and data visualization. 

### Computer Science Responsibilities: 

Following are some of the most common responsibilities in computer science roles: 

#### Writing code

As a computer science expert, you must be familiar with multiple programming languages such as Python, C#, C++, Java, and more. You’ll have to write code, test it, and debug any issues present in it. 

#### Software development

Computer science professionals develop software applications that help users perform specific tasks. They develop different components of the software and combine them together to create a fully functioning application. 

#### Collaborating on projects

In most cases, computer science professionals work in teams, and hence, they should have strong interpersonal and communication skills. They must be familiar with different collaboration and project management software such as Zoom, Todoist, and more.

Choosing between data science vs. computer science is relatively easy if you understand both of these domains. Data science is suitable for those who like to work with numbers and statistics. On the other hand, computer science and computer science jobs are more suitable for building things. Computer science roles are also ideal for studying the interactions between humans and these technologies. 

You should pick the domain that matches your career interests and aspirations. Do you find data exciting, or are you more interested in seeing how computers work?

## 5\. FAQs- Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1. Which is better – data science or computer science?

It depends on your preferences. Both data science and computer science have their unique highlights. Computer science focuses on researching abstract concepts related to software, computers, algorithms and is a more research-centric subject. On the other hand, data science focuses on combining the knowledge of different topics, including mathematics, statistics, and machine learning to find patterns in large amounts of data and analyze the same. 

Hence, computer science is better for those who want to study computers, their capabilities, and their work principles. Conversely, data science is better for those who want to solve business problems and find insights by using technology that would be invisible otherwise.
