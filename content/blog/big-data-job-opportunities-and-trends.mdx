## What Is Big Data Job?

Big data refers to collecting, storing, and working with such humongous amounts of data that traditional data management solutions can’t store or process them effectively. A big data professional is someone who can work with big data by using big data tools and technologies. While there are many roles present in this field, all of them require you to handle different types of data (structured, unstructured, and semi-structured).

Working in a big data job requires knowing how to handle an organization’s big data infrastructure and tools. You’ll be developing, maintaining, testing, and evaluating big data solutions for your company as it’s one of the most important big data job requirements in any big data job description.

![](https://www.analytixlabs.co.in/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/1.jpg)

You should be familiar with the **_[different characteristics of big data](https://www.analytixlabs.co.in/blog/characteristics-of-big-data/)_**, which are variability, volume, velocity, and variety. As a big data professional, you’ll have to manage the data stored in your organization and find ways to optimize the same. You’ll have to find ways to enhance the data streaming and analysis capabilities of your organization. 

Keep in mind that your responsibilities vary greatly according to your role. 

## Big Data Career Opportunities and Trends

Big data is a broad field, and so it has a ton of different career opportunities. The following points will illustrate the best of them: 

### Top big data career opportunities:

#### Data Analyst

A data analyst analyses the data systems present in their organization and work to enhance the same. They help the data scientist gather the required data and are responsible for retrieving information from different sources to help with the analytics processes. Data analysts are responsible for generating the required reports as well. 

#### Data Scientist

A data scientist is responsible for analyzing large amounts of data to generate insights that help the organization make decisions. They use machine learning, statistics, and various technologies to analyze the data and find insights. Data scientists share their insights with the stakeholders, including the investors and upper management. 

#### Data Engineer

A data engineer manages the data starting from the raw source and ensures that it is processed, stored, and available in a required format to meet the data science and business goals. Data engineers are leaders who handle large amounts of raw data and ensure that the big data implementations of their organization match its goals and requirements. They are responsible for evaluating new conventional and unconventional data sources too. For more in-depth understanding, you may like to read _**[What is Big Data Engineering or Modern Data Engineering?](https://www.analytixlabs.co.in/blog/what-is-data-engineering/)**_

#### Data Architect

A data architect is an expert who helps their organization to design and build the necessary big data infrastructure. They have to understand their organization’s needs to design the big data systems accordingly. ## Big Data Job Requirements

Big data roles require you to be familiar with multiple big data tools, including Apache Hadoop, MapReduce, Apache Spark, Pig, and others. These are industry staples and are necessary to store, process, and work with large quantities of data. 

To pursue job opportunities for big data analytics, you must have good programming skills as you’ll have to use Python, Java, and C++ while working with the different big data tools. Another crucial skill for this industry is machine learning, as it allows you to automate large processes and analyze data effectively. Classification, K-NN, CNN, and decision trees are some of the most popular machine learning algorithms big data experts use. 

You must also be familiar with data warehousing, and databases as storing the data is a key aspect of any big data process. You must also know how to share your insights effectively by using data visualization.
