### **AI Skills 2026 Playbook: The Continuation**

-   **What’s Changing? (2019 vs. 2026)**

The “center of gravity” for hiring has shifted from basic tools to production-grade AI.

-   **Declining Demand:** Legacy roles like basic Business Intelligence, SQL-only data roles, and Hadoop-era Big Data are seeing an oversupply of talent and declining interest from top firms.
-   **Explosive Growth:** AI and data analytics roles are growing at **30% year-on-year**, driven by the adoption of Large Language Models (LLMs) and automation.
-   **The Rise of Ethics:** AI Safety, Ethics, and Governance have climbed from near-invisibility in 2019 to a top-15 essential skill for 2025.

### **The Payoff: Salary & Career Growth**

The “AI Talent Crunch” is directly impacting compensation. While pure Machine Learning hiring may soften, salaries for senior-tier professionals with hybrid GenAI+ML responsibilities remain high.

![salary gaps by skills 2026](https://www.analytixlabs.co.in/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/Screenshot-2026-01-28-at-1.03.27-PM.png)

-   **The Elite Tier:** Senior specialists in LLM and GenAI roles at Tier-1 firms are seeing median salaries in the **28–32 LPA** range.
-   **Closing the Gap:** By 2027, the gender pay gap in AI is projected to fall below 10% as more women enter high-tech specializations like MLOps and Infrastructure AI.
