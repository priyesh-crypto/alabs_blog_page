The rise of growing concern among users regarding privacy has led governments from different countries to devise regulations to check the misuse of users’ information. While the European Union introduced the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), India tabled Personal Data Protection Bill (PDP) 2019 on 11 December last year in the Lok Sabha. The idea behind such bills is to fortify the exploitation of critical information of citizens at the same time allowing Analytics professionals to make solutions leveraging data for simplifying the life of users.

However, to ensure organizations do not misuse users’ data, the Indian government has included laws that will penalize the solution providers in case of data privacy breaches. Consequently, data-driven firms are getting critical of the ways information is being used at every step of the development cycle. This has led the firms to hire business analyst professionals who can ensure compliance while making business strategies using users’ information.

The datasets, if provided by them, can be used by city planners to devise a healthy flow of traffic in the Indian cities. Other facets of implementation can be seen in other Indian sectors like health care. So, organizations must focus on the developing requirements – this also where Business Analysts enter the stage.

### **Driving Insights With Data To Make Public Schemes**

PDP will act as a foundation for the government and companies alike in building products while abiding by the rules. This will bring trust among users about how the data is being used. Such a framework will enable organizations in collaboration with governments to derive insights into non-personal data to provide various public services. For instance, the healthcare industry can use data from electronic health records (EHRs) to find insights and determine trends, which can then be used for assisting the government in making public health schemes based on rising health issues.

Today, to deliver such insights and trends, companies need to ensure that data is being used in a way that doesn’t breach the privacy of users. Therefore, organizations are looking for proficient business analytics professionals to streamline their workflows and generate meaningful insights from the data available.

India is slowly becoming the repository for the analytics discipline. With a market pool that makes India the second-biggest analytics hubs followed by the US, it is clear that this figure will double to [2 lakhs from 97,000 in 2020](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/industry/services/education/indias-advanced-analytics-pool-to-triple-by-2020-bain-company/articleshow/70159874.cms?from=mdr). India also accounts for one in 10 advanced business analytics job profiles across the globe.

Analytics discipline is garnering its much-needed traction with the dominant adulation of business analytics in India’s core industries – triggering a hiring spree in IT, Finance, and Start-Ups. As the tech wind grows exponentially, more **scope of business analytics** is generated. ### **Organizations Can Make Use Of Data Held By Governments**

Every second, a colossal amount of data is being created, and it is envisioned that information will only grow in the coming years. Organizations are moulding users’ data for gaining insights and providing bespoke solutions to diverse customers’ needs. Thus, data has become the driving force for businesses right from finance to retail and healthcare sectors.

Organizations can today make use of government data for creating effective strategies and public schemes around data. For one, tech companies can use transportation data available with governments to optimize the schedule of public buses and reduce traffic congestion in cities. Besides, financial data from public banks can be used with machine learning models to quickly open up the line of credit for users by analysing customers’ historical data, thereby eliminating the need for dated pen and paper approach.

According to the World Economic Forum, it is estimated that every day 463 exabytes of information will be generated globally by 2025. To handle and analyse a plethora of data, governments and organizations will have to work in tandem to gain insights and making informed decisions. Consequently, organizations are seeking business analysts who can assist them in making new strategies that would bring value to users at the same time help in business growth. Therefore, obtaining business intelligence knowledge is of paramount importance for aspirants to receive desired job offers.

The accompanying question post-PDP era should not fixate on “**what is business analytics?”** but more on what’s in it. As the government aims to use private data to shape public policy, the private sectors are going to play a key role in this reformation. Companies like Uber, Ola, Google are storehouses of immense data sets that reflect on the literal landscape of Indian cities.

Consumers will always look for the next-big-think when they have options to choose from. Such an instance leaves a void which can be compensated by BA with their tools and statistics to decode key insights. So, Business Analysts are the key players in keeping the concerned companies retain a competitive edge across industries.
