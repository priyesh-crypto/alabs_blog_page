## Chapter 02: How Jobs Have Evolved with the Onset of GenAI?

The work landscape is undergoing a seismic shift as generative AI advancements redefine job roles, industries, and daily routines. This is also a consequence of Indian Enterprises’ growing adoption of AI. 

The AI adoption Index from 2022 to 2024 has evolved from strategy to execution. What was once about vision, budget, tech readiness, and risks now focuses on AI lifecycle maturity, skills, use cases, and budget. 

### 02.01. India’s AI Maturity

## Chapter 03: Analyzing the Growing AI Talent Gap in India

According to recent research, the impact of AI on the overall job market will look like this by 2025: 

-   New jobs created due to AI: 97 million
-   Old jobs lost due to AI: 85 million

Bridging the AI skill gap is urgent. As more investments are made in AI-driven technologies, companies will open up roles that require core AI skills. 

This stature applies to all industries, including the data science and analytics market. Integrating data with AI is the base on which these advanced systems function.

While traditional data knowledge is essential to building such technologies, it also requires professionals to be conversant with AI, why it is taking over the market, and what powers it harnesses to create a robust, scalable, and valuable system.

As more companies embrace AI, the need for data scientists who understand its workings and ethics and data science concepts is increasing.

_**The problem is that data scientists still need to become skilled in AI intelligence.**_

Another major point is that data science requires a highly experienced professional. A 30-year experienced professional is seldom heard of in the current date. Even if you find a professional with 10+ years of experience professional, they have a high salary demand.

The gap is not just in the need for more skilled professionals but also experience-wise. Skilled professionals in the 0-6 years of band are hard to find and most in demand.

## Chapter 05: The GenAI-led Data Science Hiring Scenario in India

The talent crunch is as real as it can get in India. This directly impacts how enterprises approach AI for their organizations. 

> Building expertise in-house is relatively challenging because companies need help hiring and retaining talent over a considerable period of time. 

![ai hiring challenges](https://www.analytixlabs.co.in/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/Screenshot-2024-11-18-at-12.37.46 PM.png)

Due to the field’s novelty, hiring dedicated AI leadership teams is extremely difficult. Only some professionals have more than five years of experience in AI alone to take over leadership roles. As a result, companies are relying on IT heads to own AI initiatives with ad hoc execution teams. 

-   Research shows that only 8% of companies in the mature stage of AI adoption have designated AI leaders. These professionals are responsible for envisioning and leading enterprise-scale AI initiatives in an ineffective and agile way. 
-   Only 13% of enterprises have dedicated AI teams in India, pinpointing the glaring talent crunch in AI. 

Yes, companies are investing heavily in AI, but when it comes to compliance and AI ethics, Indian enterprises’ numbers have yet to improve.

Generative AI and Data Science are two powerful fields. However, what’s interesting is that they complement each other, can drive innovation, and can solve complex problems (across various domains) together.

Data Science is a multidisciplinary field that combines statistics, mathematics, computer science, storytelling, business acumen, and domain expertise to extract insights from various kinds of data. 

Due to the exponential growth in data generation due to digital devices and online activities, data science has become indispensable across numerous sectors, including banking, finance, healthcare, and marketing.

The synergy between Data Science and Gen AI has the potential to lead to a creative and collaborative approach. Thanks to Gen AI, Data scientists can now concentrate on the “why” and “how” of data analysis while Gen AI deals with the “what”. 

This collaboration allows data scientists to understand the data and the phenomena it represents, enhance decision-making, automate tasks, and drive innovation, pushing boundaries of what is possible with data science.

As companies prepare to reskill and upskill existing talents, it is also a wake-up call for learning professionals to embrace core AI skills that work in tandem with data science skills.

### 06.03. Data Scientist vs. GenAI – Who Will Replace What?

A common misconception is that Gen AI will take over data scientists. While it’s true that Gen AI can automate repetitive data science tasks, it is unlikely to replace data scientists. Several human skills, such as strategic thinking, creativity, ethics, and communication, remain vital and are not a forte of Gen AI.

Generative AI, on the other hand, can help augment data scientists’ productivity, enabling them to focus on more critical tasks like high-value analysis and strategic thinking.
