## What Are the Different Job Profiles of the Data Science Industry?

The Data Science industry has a plethora of data science opportunities and there are many jobs after data science course. It’s a broad sector and so requires professionals with various expertise to ensure everything functions properly. The most prominent Data Science jobs are as follows:

### Data Analyst

Data analysts work with large quantities of data and handle the pre-processing and processing tasks of the same. They are responsible for optimizing the data so it would generate valuable and actionable insights with minimal error.

### Data Architect

Data architects create the necessary blueprints for their organizations to integrate, centralize and protect their databases. They are responsible for providing the data engineers of the organization with the optimal systems and tools. Data architects must be adept in data modeling, data warehousing, and related concepts.

### Data Engineer

A Data Engineer builds and tests big data ecosystems for their organization so that the Data Scientists can make use of the data and run the required algorithms. **_[Modern Data Engineering](https://www.analytixlabs.co.in/blog/what-is-data-engineering/)_** is responsible for the performance of the existing Big Data systems and must keep them updated with newer or upgraded versions. They use NoSQL, C++, Matlab, Hive, and several other big data technologies to perform their duties.

### Data Scientist

A data scientist helps a business understand its problems and offers solutions by using data processing and analysis. They perform various tasks, including data cleaning, research, data wrangling, and data visualization to generate insights their business can use to improve their operations or overcome a particular challenge.

## Is Data Scientist an IT Job

Many people get confused about the nature of a data scientist’s job. As it’s an interdisciplinary role, people get confused about whether data scientist jobs would fall under the category of IT jobs or not.

### What defines an IT job?

IT is an abbreviation for Information Technology. Most IT jobs are a sort of technical support job, i.e., they help other departments of their organization in using particular technologies. Some of the most popular IT jobs are Programmer Analyst, Computer Consultant, Chief Information Officer (CIO), Systems Administrator, etc. Their primary duty is to help the staff of their organization in using and utilizing a particular technology. Many staff members don’t know how to use every technology that their organization uses. This is where an IT professional comes into play. They help the staff members in understanding, using, and fixing the relevant technologies.

For example, a network administrator would be responsible for maintaining and using all the network servers present in a particular department of an organization.

### Data Scientist is an IT enabled job

Based on the definition we discussed above, can we call a data scientist job an ITES job, i.e., an IT-enabled job. Like most IT jobs focus on helping their organization using a particular technology, Data Scientists focus on helping their organization use Data. They are experts in handling large amounts of data and are responsible for deriving business value.

Just as a database administrator helps different staff members use and manage the organization’s database systems, a Data Scientist helps the organization manage its data and develop smarter solutions. They help the leaders of their company in making better-informed decisions by leveraging insights from the available data.

Data scientists must be proficient in many aspects of information technology, including programming and computer science, to perform their duties properly. Without a good familiarity with the basics of IT, they wouldn’t be able to work effectively.

This is a prominent reason why most companies that offer a Data Scientist job for freshers would mention it in their IT section.

## Which Is an Important Language to Learn to Start a Data Scientist Career?

Data scientists must know multiple programming languages to perform their various duties. They gather, analyze, and clean data. Moreover, they also have to organize the data properly, remain easily accessible, and share their insights with other team members. The most important programming languages that a data scientist must know are:

### Python

Python is among the most popular programming languages on the globe. It is also one of the most popular languages among data scientists. Many organizations list Python as a must-have whenever they post a data scientist job for freshers. Python is widely popular among data science professionals due to its easy-to-learn syntax and versatile libraries.

You can **_[master Python for Data Science](https://www.analytixlabs.co.in/blog/mastering-python-for-data-science/)_** without having any coding experience due to its simplicity. You’d need to use Python libraries in machine learning, natural data processing, and deep learning. Numpy, Scipy, Keras, Scikit-Learn, Pandas, and TensorFlow are the most popular Python libraries that data scientists use.

### R

R is another widely popular programming language that data scientists use. The primary applications of R in data science are data analysis, data cleaning, data import and export, and statistical analysis.

Apart from these uses, data scientists use R for statistical analysis and predictive modeling. R has a ton of applications in data science; however, it has an easier learning curve compared to Python.
