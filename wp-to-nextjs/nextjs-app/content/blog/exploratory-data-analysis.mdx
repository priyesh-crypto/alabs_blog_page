## What is Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)?

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is among the initial and most critical analytical processes. In this process, analysts examine datasets to understand their information, structure, and any problems that need attention. This approach provides a highly granular view of data quality, distribution, and relationships among variables, depending on the level of EDA performed.

This form of analytical process involves collecting, profiling, and sometimes even learning a dataset to reveal trends, anomalies, missing values, etc., before any kind of advanced modelling building can begin.

Analysts use several tools and techniques during EDA, including:

-   **Summary statistics** such as mean, median, and standard deviation.
-   **Inferential statistics** using hypothesis tests like the Z-test, t-test, ANOVA, and chi-square.
-   **Data visualizations** using graphs like scatter plots and histograms.

These methods help identify patterns and potential issues in the data.

Long story short,

> Exploratory Data Analysis is essentially data exploration that helps you understand the data at hand and identify issues. It kick-starts the process of transforming raw information into organized insights, ensuring that subsequent analyses rest on accurate and trustworthy data foundations.

Now, let us look at how EDA gets involved in various aspects of data analytics and data science. 

### Purpose of Exploratory Analysis in Data Analytics

EDA serves different purposes in different fields. Let’s first answer the purpose of exploratory analysis in data analytics. The purpose of EDA in data analytics is to understand and prepare the dataset for insights. This ensures that business decisions rely on facts, not assumptions or gut feelings.

In this stage, analysts test data integrity and identify trends. They also uncover hidden relationships that guide decision-making. Common tasks include detecting missing values and spotting outliers. Analysts also study correlations in the data. They verify whether the data meets business needs and analytical goals. This happens before the data is used for advanced analytics.

The key point is that EDA in data analytics focuses on diagnostics and data understanding. It reveals what the data shows about current or past events.

Let’s understand this with a practical example.

A retail firm is exploring sales data. They employ EDA to uncover seasonal purchase patterns or anomalies in certain regions. The information gained from EDA will help them guide marketing strategies and operational decisions. 

### EDA in Data Science

Lets now focus on the question – what is EDA in data science?

Data scientists spend [80% of their time](https://www.forbes.com/sites/gilpress/2016/03/23/data-preparation-most-time-consuming-least-enjoyable-data-science-task-survey-says/) cleaning and exploring data. This statistic emphasizes how critical EDA in Data Science is. While data exploration in data analytics focuses on understanding data for decision-making, EDA in data science goes a step further because it lays the groundwork for performing predictive modeling and machine learning.

Data scientists use EDA to validate assumptions, test variable interactions, and ensure the dataset’s suitability for algorithms. This process bridges the gap between raw data and model design, ensuring that every feature used in modeling is relevant.

The term “Exploratory Data Analysis” was coined by John Tukey in the 1970s, who emphasized methodologically exploring data rather than merely using statistical methods.

In modern practice, EDA in data science includes statistical summaries, visual diagnostics, and hypothesis validation to ensure models are trained on meaningful and unbiased data. Skipping this phase can have severe consequences.

The criticality of EDA in data science is emphasized by the fact that [85% of failed AI projects](https://www.forbes.com/councils/forbestechcouncil/2024/11/15/why-85-of-your-ai-models-may-fail/) stem from not understanding your data fully..

Hence, analytics-oriented EDA focuses on understanding and interpreting data. Data science-driven EDA ensures model integrity by checking that all model assumptions are fulfilled.

### EDA in Machine Learning (Relevance of EDA before modeling)

Lastly, let’s expand on what EDA means in machine learning. By answering this, you can understand how EDA acts as an iterative bridge between raw data and machine learning models. With each iteration, model readiness improves as new insights emerge and more inconsistencies come to light. Technically, effective EDA enhances model reliability and business interpretability. It also reduces risks of overfitting, bias, and false accuracy.

## Exploratory Data Types and Techniques

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is the stage where analysts understand the data’s structure and uncover patterns. But data scientists go a step beyond and assess data quality before formally starting model building.

### Types of Exploratory Data Analysis

No matter who is performing for what purpose, the EDA methods fall into three major categories: **Univariate**, **Bivariate**, and **Multivariate**, with each addressing the data’s complexity from a different lens.

![types of exploratory data analysis](https://www.analytixlabs.co.in/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/What-is-Exploratory-Data-Analysis-02.jpg)

#### 1\. Univariate Analysis

Univariate Analysis focuses on examining a single variable at a time to understand its distribution, central tendency, variability, etc. Such an analysis answers questions like “What is the typical value?” “Are there outliers or missing values in this variable?”, “What is the distribution of the variable?”.

The common descriptive measures include mean, median, mode, variance, standard deviation, skewness, kurtosis, etc. Visual methods like histograms, frequency bar plots, and boxplots are also used. For instance, a histogram of customer ages might reveal that most customers fall between 25 and 40 years, while boxplots show any extreme ages that may distort averages. This analysis is considered important because it highlights the fundamental characteristics of the dataset without considering relationships to other variables.

#### 2\. Bivariate Analysis

The next kind is bivariate analysis, which explores the relationship between two variables, revealing whether they are correlated, dependent, or unrelated. Typical questions answered during such analysis include “Does advertising influence sales?” or “Is there a link between income and education level?”

Tools such as scatter plots, pair plots, bar charts, and correlation matrices are used to visualize patterns and measure associations. In addition, statistical measures like correlation coefficients, t-tests, or chi-square tests are used to quantify those relationships further. For example, a positive correlation between ad spend and revenue suggests that higher marketing investment likely drives sales growth.

#### 3\. Multivariate Analysis

Multivariate Analysis is the most complex one as it examines more than two variables simultaneously to understand their combined influence. During such an analysis, interdependencies and patterns are identified that might not be visible through single or two-variable views or analyses. Common methods include Principal Component Analysis (PCA) for dimension reduction, Recursive Feature Elimination for feature selection, heatmaps for correlation clusters, 3D scatter plots for multi-factor visualization, etc.

This form of analysis is vital in complex domains such as healthcare or finance, where outcomes depend on numerous interacting factors. PCA, for instance, helps condense hundreds of features into a few latent dimensions that explain most data variance and allows for complex model building and visualization.

For each analytical type (univariate, bivariate, and multivariate), the corresponding visual and statistical tools are different. Thus, **all three analytical types can be explored using either _graphical_ or _non-graphical_ approaches**.

-   **Non-Graphical EDA** summarizes data numerically using summary statistics such as mean, median, standard deviation, and variance, or explores the relationship between variables and transforms them using hypothesis tests and different statistical techniques.
-   **Graphical EDA** complements numbers with visualization by using different kinds of charts to reveal hidden trends or anomalies that numbers alone may conceal.

Typically, both these types are used together, as they create a balanced perspective: numeric summaries confirm the magnitude of relationships, while visuals show their patterns.

### Core Techniques of Each Type of EDA

Below is a list of the most prominent techniques used during EDA.

![](https://www.analytixlabs.co.in/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/What-is-Exploratory-Data-Analysis-03.jpg)

#### 1\. Univariate non-graphical techniques

These use only one variable and rely on statistical summaries:

-   Central Tendency Measures – Mean, median, and mode describe the dataset’s typical value.
-   Spread Measures – Variance and standard deviation quantify dispersion.
-   Shape Descriptors – Skewness shows asymmetry, while kurtosis indicates the “peakedness” of the distribution. Analysts can also detect outliers through z-scores or interquartile range (IQR).

#### 2\. Univariate graphical techniques

Common visualization tools used to uncover single-variable patterns are:

-   Histograms to show distribution and frequency.
-   Boxplots to display median, quartiles, and outliers.
-   Stem-and-Leaf Plots to present actual values while showing shape.
-   QQ Plots to assess normality.
-   Frequency Bar Plot is used to present the distribution of different categories of a categorical variable.

Each of these visual techniques helps to quickly expose skewness, multimodality, or abnormal values.

#### 3\. Bivariate and multivariate non-graphical techniques

When studying variable interactions, you can use:

-   Cross-Tabulation (Two-Way Tables) for categorical variables.
-   Descriptive Statistics by Group, e.g., comparing average salary by department.
-   Correlation and Covariance Matrices to quantify the strength of relationships.

All these numeric tools help to identify associations before deeper modeling.

#### 4\. Bivariate and multivariate graphical techniques

Visual tools typically used to illustrate complex relationships effectively are as follows:

-   Scatter Plots – Display correlation between two quantitative variables.
-   Run Charts and Line Graphs – Track variable behavior over time.
-   Heatmaps – Use color to represent correlation strength.
-   Bubble Charts and Multivariate Charts – Show three or more dimensions at once, (often by indicating a third variable by bubble size, color, or shape).
-   Grouped Bar Charts – Compare categories across multiple variables.

All these visuals are critical for simplifying the comprehension of multi-variable dependencies.

#### Supplementary EDA Techniques

In addition to visualization and techniques mentioned so far, analysts employ various statistical tests such as t-tests, ANOVA, and chi-square to validate observed differences, and outlier detection using boxplots or z-scores to ensure data integrity. For high-dimensional datasets, clustering and dimension-reduction (like K-means or PCA) are also used to simplify analysis while preserving relationships.

### How to Choose the Right Technique?

While there are many techniques, it’s critical to know which technique to use when. A great way to guide yourself to choose an appropriate EDA technique is to focus on your analytical goal.

-   When exploring single variables, start with univariate numerical and graphical methods.
-   For relationships, progress to bivariate EDA.
-   When dealing with complex systems, adopt multivariate EDA with visual and statistical integration.

## What is AI-assisted Exploratory Data Analysis?

AI-Assisted Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) integrates artificial intelligence and machine learning into traditional data exploration. It automates manual steps such as profiling, visualization, missing value, and anomaly detection, while at the same time enabling analysts to query data in natural language and receive instant statistical or graphical insights. The combination of automation and intelligence is lethal as an AI-assisted EDA  due to the following reasons.

-   **Faster**: AI EDA automates profiling, which allows for instant summarization of datasets, visualizations generation, and highlighting missing or inconsistent values, thereby reducing manual work. Given that it can convert raw data into structured, interpretable insights within minutes rather than hours, it can drastically increase the efficiency of data analysts and scientists.
-   **Smarter**: Machine-learning algorithms can identify patterns, detect outliers, and suggest imputation or transformation strategies not only quickly but more accurately. In addition, natural-language features let users ask questions like “What drives customer churn?” and receive contextual answers almost immediately.
-   **More scalable**: Lastly, AI-driven pipelines have the ability to handle terabytes of data, making EDA repeatable and real-time across departments.

Given all these advantages, there has been a massive rise in AI-assisted EDA tools. The following are the **key tools that you should be aware of.**

### i. Tools for Data Scientists (Python users)

These tools/libraries suit programmers who prefer control through code but want to automate exploratory tasks. They integrate seamlessly with Pandas dataframes and Jupyter notebooks, generating reports or plots with minimal commands.

-   ydata-profiling / pandas-profiling – Produces detailed descriptive statistics, correlations, and warnings in a single HTML report.
-   Sweetviz – Delivers target-based visualizations and dataset comparisons in seconds, useful for model preparation.
-   AutoViz, Lux, DataPrep, D-Tale, dabl – Great for automating chart generation, feature analysis, and interactive browsing.

### ii. Tools for Analysts and Business Teams

The tools mentioned below cater to non-technical users who depend on intuitive dashboards rather than code. They integrate AI and natural-language querying to uncover insights visually. Such tools democratize analytics by allowing business users to conduct EDA directly.

-   Tableau (Tableau GPT / Pulse) – Recommends visuals, interprets metrics, and personalizes dashboards using embedded AI. 
-   Power BI (Copilot) – Enables natural-language queries and automated visuals within the Microsoft 365 ecosystem.

Qlik- Uses an associative engine and AI recommendations to expose hidden relationships between variables.  

### iii. Tools for Enterprises

Enterprise-level AI EDA tools combine automation, scalability, governance, and machine-learning integration. Such tools are designed and built for scale, managing complex data ecosystems and regulatory oversight, which ensures high performance, transparency, and compliance across enterprise-scale environments**.**

-   DataRobot – Automates data preparation, EDA, and model training within an auditable pipeline.
-   H2O.ai – Provides open-source and enterprise AutoML that scales EDA and modeling across distributed environments.
-   Google Cloud AutoML / Vertex AI – Integrates AI-assisted profiling and model development directly into cloud-native pipelines for continuous, organization-wide insight generation.

## Real-world Use Cases of Exploratory Data Analysis

EDA is used in numerous domains because almost all domains deal with raw data, and EDA is critical for transforming such data into actionable information. Below are some of the key sectors where EDA is commonly used.

### 1\. Retail and eCommerce

EDA is used to identify buying patterns, seasonal demand, and basket affinities, which are used to drive assortment, pricing, and promotional decisions.

### 2\. FinTech and risk analytics 

EDA helps in performing transaction profiling, time-series trend checks, and outlier detection, which are all critical for enabling early fraud flags, real-time monitoring, and risk scoring. Behavioural clustering and credit risk segmentation are also dependent on effective EDA.

### 3\. Healthcare and Biosciences

EDA is critical in revealing correlations across patient demographics, test results, and outcomes, supporting disease-prediction modelling, and ensuring data quality before clinical analysis. 

### 4\. Marketing

Several tasks are to be performed in the marketing domain, which include customer segmentation, campaign effect, channel allocation, personalization etc. Various EDA techniques, such as correlations, A/B tests, and heatmaps, form the backbone to perform all such tasks. 

### 5\. Manufacturing and Quality Control

By performing EDA on sensor and process data, the analysts can reveal drift, batch anomalies, early warning signals for predictive maintenance, etc.

### 6\. HR analytics and people science

EDA is critical in assessing attrition, promotion timelines, engagement survey data, etc, which is critical for workforce planning, informed retention experiments, etc.

### 7\. Sports Analytics

Sports analytics is a niche but highly sought-after field and there are ​several uses of exploratory data analysis in this field​. EDA plays a critical role in sports analytics by performing tasks like match-level and player-level visualisations, which are great for uncovering performance drivers, supporting selection decisions, and informing tactical changes. EDA is also used today in fantasy/sports-betting analyses by exposing hidden patterns, allowing users to make better decisions.

### 8\. Environmental and energy analytics

Exploratory Data Analysis gets involved in this domain through time-series exploration of weather, demand, and emissions data. By identifying seasonality, anomalies, and efficiency opportunities in this data, users can perform grid planning, renewables integration, and conservation programs.

### 9\. Cybersecurity and fraud/risk detection

By effectively using EDA, analysts can perform tasks like profiling, clustering, and anomaly detection and its visualization, all of which help in detecting unusual login patterns, fraudulent transactions, suspicious data inflows, etc.

### 10\. Geospatial and transportation

Spatial EDA is particularly used in this domain to expose congestion hotspots, route inefficiencies, and region-level demand clusters. By combining maps with time series data exploration, you can perform logistics optimisation and urban planning.

### 11\. Indian Government

Governments and citizens all around the world use EDA to make sense of the government-collected data effectively, and the Indian government is no different. Several public platforms have made government datasets usable for exploratory analysis. For instance, NDAP (NITI Aayog’s National Data and Analytics Platform) provides [interoperable, documented datasets](https://ndap.niti.gov.in/) along with built-in visualization/merge tools to enable cross-sectoral EDA, which is great for policy making and research (As of May 2022[, NDAP hosted 203 datasets](https://egovernance.vikaspedia.in/viewcontent/e-governance/digital-india/national-data-and-analytics-platform?lgn=en) across 14 sectors, and this number has grown ever since).

EDA is an extremely practical technique as it is used to convert distributions into resource allocations, correlations into marketing and product strategies, anomalies into risk alerts, and patterns into operational efficiencies. It, along with AI-assisted tools, helps to greatly reduce the journey from raw data to actionable insight, which supports evidence-based decision-making across both business operations and public policy development.

Given the rapid growth of data volume, velocity, and variety, EDA has become extremely critical in ensuring that downstream models and decisions do not rely on flawed, biased, or noisy inputs.  Now, if you have gotten some idea regarding how EDA works in the real world, then let’s understand how the EDA outcomes above (patterns, correlations, anomalies, and distributions) are converted into features used for modelling and operational decision-making.

## From EDA to Actions: Turning Insights into Features

EDA not only identifies the relationships, anomalies, and trends in the data but is also used to enrich the data by shaping predictive features, which are the inputs that power machine learning models. Therefore, in today’s ML-AI-centric world, EDA is no longer just a diagnostic step but has become the foundation for every mode-building exercise.

Now, before getting into specifics, let’s understand how EDA converts insights into features. As you would have seen by now, EDA detects patterns, correlations, anomalies, and distributions among other things that define a dataset’s behavior. All these insights can be converted into features, i.e., quantifiable attributes that ML models can use to predict outcomes or classify events. Let’s look at a few insights provided by EDA and how they are converted to attain meaningful features.

-   **Patterns**: recurring behaviors or seasonality evolve into frequency or temporal features.
-   **Correlations**: relationships between variables can become ratio or interaction features.
-   **Anomalies**: outlier patterns get translated into binary flags or distance metrics for fraud detection.

Thus, by turning observations into structured, model-ready variables, EDA becomes critical in transforming insights into something that the algorithms can act upon. Now, let’s look at some particular cases.

![eda use cases](https://www.analytixlabs.co.in/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/What-is-Exploratory-Data-Analysis-04.jpg)

### 1\. Customer Behaviour Analysis

EDA reveals how customers spend, browse, and engage across channels. Distributions of spend, session time, and purchase frequency are critical as they become segmentation features. Such features enhance models’ predicting capabilities, especially where personalization, preferred marketing channels, demand, etc, are to be predicted.

### 2\. Product Optimisation

EDA highlights performance variations across SKUs, geographies, and configurations. Correlation insights on price, design, and usage are translated into quality and performance features that enhance the ML model’s capabilities for accurately predicting product success, pricing, R&D allocation, etc.

### 3\. Risk and Fraud Detection

Anomalies in transaction amounts, timing, or device patterns are critical information provided by EDA that become risk features that strengthen fraud detection models.

### 4\. Operational Efficiency

EDA quantifies fluctuations in production or logistics into variance features for predictive maintenance and process optimization. Thus, by strengthening predictive models, organizations can minimize downtime and improve resource utilization.

### 5\. Sales and Revenue Growth

EDA can be used to derive lag and seasonality features from time-series sales data. Such features help models to forecast demand and optimize pricing and inventory strategies.

### 6\. Churn and Retention

Activity drop-offs and sentiment shifts are critical information provided by EDA, which helps in creating churn-risk indicators for retention models.

### 7\. Market Expansion

EDA can be used to convert demographic and transactional data into geospatial features. Such features are extremely important to create ML models that identify untapped markets and guide policy or investment decisions.

Across all domains, EDA is used to ensure that statistically sound and contextually relevant features can be created. Whether it’s identifying customer intent or mitigating risks, EDA helps in converting information gained from data exploration into structured features that enhance the intelligence of ML models. In an era of data abundance, this conversion is what is critical in turning analysis into something that predictive models can rely upon and provide a measurable impact.

## FAQs

-   **What are the steps in Data Exploration?**

Typical steps performed during data exploration involve data collection, cleaning, transformation, visualization, and statistical summarization to understand structure, detect anomalies, and prepare for modeling.

-   **Why is Exploratory Data Analysis important?**

EDA is important because it ensures data quality, exposes hidden patterns, validates assumptions, and guides feature engineering, making all subsequent tasks, such as model building, reliable.

-   **What is EDA in machine learning?**

In machine learning, EDA is responsible for identifying important variables, relationships between them, and transforming them to improve the predictive accuracy of the ML model.

-   **What is the purpose of data visualization in EDA?**

Data visualization translates raw numbers into visual patterns, which not only helps to make an intuitive sense of numbers but also helps in revealing trends, correlations, and anomalies that may not be so evident in tables.

-   **Can EDA be automated?**

Yes, AI-assisted tools like YData-Profiling, Sweetviz, and AutoViz help to automate EDA.

-   **Why is Exploratory Data Analysis important in business applications?**

EDA helps businesses detect risk, optimize operations, predict customer behavior, and turn data-driven insights into strategic decisions.

-   **What are the three objectives of data exploration?**

The three primary objectives are understanding data structure, discovering relationships and trends, and validating assumptions before modeling.

-   **What are the Best Practices for Conducting EDA?**

One should start by defining the business problem, followed by assessing data quality, picking the right EDA technique as per the requirement, iterating and refining the EDA process, documenting steps, and integrating domain expertise in the analysis.
