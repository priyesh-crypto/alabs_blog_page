## **Top Sectors Hiring Data Scientists in Hyderabad (and Why They Are Booming)**

Hyderabad’s data science hiring is not limited to IT services. The city’s economy spans pharma, banking, semiconductors, and aerospace. Each sector uses data scientists differently. Here is what you can expect.

![data scientist jobs sectors](https://www.analytixlabs.co.in/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Screenshot-2026-04-13-at-11.41.25-AM.png)

### **IT Services and GCCs**

IT services account for roughly 63% of data scientist job postings in Hyderabad on Naukri. Companies like Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and Salesforce operate large development and analytics centres in HITEC City and Raheja Mindspace. In these roles, you will work on product analytics, recommendation systems, and ML engineering. Many GCCs have moved beyond support functions into global product ownership where you build models that serve millions of users worldwide.

### **Healthcare and Life Sciences**

This sector represents about 8.6% of postings. Hyderabad is India’s pharmaceutical capital, anchored by Genome Valley. Companies like Novartis, Sanofi, Dr. Reddy’s, and Eli Lilly run R&D centres here. As a data scientist in this sector, you will work on clinical data analysis, drug discovery modelling, and bioinformatics. The convergence of pharma infrastructure and data science talent has made Hyderabad uniquely positioned for these roles.

### **Banking, Financial Services, and Insurance (BFSI)**

Although, BFSI accounts for about 5.4% of listings, these roles tend to pay well. Companies like JP Morgan Chase, HSBC, Wells Fargo, and MetLife all run analytics centres here. You get to work on risk modelling, fraud detection, regulatory tech, and customer analytics. Add to this, Hyderabad’s deep BFSI talent pool is another reason why global banks keep expanding here. This means, the future holds more job scope in this city. 

### **Emerging Sectors**

Semiconductors (Qualcomm, AMD, Intel), aerospace (Boeing), and automotive (Bosch, Hyundai, ZF) are few of the growing employers. In these roles, you apply data science to yield analysis, simulation modelling, and embedded systems optimization. These sectors are smaller in volume but often pay premium salaries for niche skills.

## **Skills You Need to Land a Data Scientist Job in Hyderabad**

-   ### **Technical Skills That Appear in 80%+ of Job Descriptions**
    

You will not get shortlisted without the core technical skills. Every data scientist job description in Hyderabad lists some combination of the following.

-   **Python:** This is the most [in-demand programming language for data science](https://www.analytixlabs.co.in/course/data-science-using-python/). You use it to clean data, build models, and automate workflows. Libraries like Pandas, NumPy, and scikit-learn are your daily tools.
-   **SQL:** This is non-negotiable. You use SQL to extract data from production databases. If you cannot write joins, window functions, and aggregations confidently, you are not ready to apply.
-   **Machine Learning:** You need working knowledge of supervised methods (regression, decision trees, ensemble models) and unsupervised methods (clustering, PCA). Frameworks like scikit-learn, TensorFlow, and PyTorch appear frequently.
-   **Data Visualization:** Power BI and Tableau are standard. You use these tools to present findings to stakeholders who do not read code.
-   **Cloud Platforms:** Azure skills are especially valued in Hyderabad because of Microsoft’s large GCC. AWS and GCP also appear regularly. Cloud proficiency adds roughly 31% to your salary potential.
-   **Statistics and Probability:** You need a solid grasp of hypothesis testing, distributions, and regression analysis. These form the backbone of every model you build.

### **Non-Technical Skills That Set You Apart**

_**Technical skills can get you shortlisted, but domain knowledge gets you hired.**_

Employers in Hyderabad’s pharma and BFSI sectors prioritize candidates who understand the business context. Your ability to frame a problem clearly, communicate findings to non-technical stakeholders, and translate insights into business action is what separates you from other applicants. This, in combination with sound knowledge of technical skills, keeps you ahead of the curve.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

### **How Do You Get a Data Scientist Job in Hyderabad as a Fresher?**

Build your foundations in Python, SQL, and machine learning first. Complete two to three portfolio projects that demonstrate your ability to clean data, build models, and communicate insights. Apply through Naukri, LinkedIn, and company career pages. A structured certification from a recognised institute strengthens your profile, especially when recruiters receive 500+ applications per role.

### **What Is the Average Data Scientist Salary in Hyderabad in 2026?**

The average falls between 12 and 14.5 LPA, depending on the source. Glassdoor reports 13.5 LPA as the mean, with a typical range of 7.87 to 19 LPA. Top earners at MNCs can reach 28+ LPA. Total compensation at top-tier companies like Salesforce averages over 56 LPA according to Levels.fyi.

### **Is Hyderabad Better Than Bengaluru for Data Science Jobs?**

It depends on what you optimize for. Bengaluru has more total listings and a larger startup ecosystem. Hyderabad is the fastest-growing GCC hub with comparable MNC salaries and significantly lower living costs. If you want the best salary-to-cost ratio with strong long-term job growth, Hyderabad is a compelling choice.

### **Which Sectors Hire the Most Data Scientists in Hyderabad?**

IT services and GCCs lead with roughly 63% of postings. Healthcare and life sciences follow at about 8.6%, driven by Hyderabad’s pharma infrastructure. BFSI accounts for 5.4%. Emerging sectors like semiconductors, aerospace, and automotive are smaller in volume but growing fast.

### **Do You Need a Degree in Computer Science to Become a Data Scientist?**

No. Many working data scientists hold degrees in mathematics, statistics, economics, engineering, and even non-technical fields. What matters is demonstrable skill in Python, SQL, statistics, and machine learning. A strong portfolio backed by practical projects carries more weight than a specific degree title.
