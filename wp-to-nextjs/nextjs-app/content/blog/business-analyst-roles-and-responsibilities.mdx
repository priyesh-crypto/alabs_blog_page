A Business Analyst, in the simplest words, is a person who deals with business connections with the data. This data can be the company’s old marketing, customer, sales, or finance records. Or, it can be that of a competitor. One of the business analyst job responsibilities is to report or provide insights based on the available data, using techniques like critical thinking, problem-solving solutions, and analysis to help a business reach its end goal. His key roles in the company comprise – Data, Decision, Performance, and Prediction.

Often, people confuse a business analyst of the analytics domain with that of IT. However, they are like two sides of the same coin. Therefore, it is crucial to understand that in the Analytics domain, a Business Analyst creates a statistical representation of numerical data by analyzing it and giving a visual touch to this data for better understanding. **To sum up** **the Business Analyst job responsibilities:**

-   Collecting information about the target audience through [surveys](https://www.jotform.com/surveys/) and studying the historical or research available regarding the same field.
-   Making feasible decisions based on the behavioral pattern of customers to comprehend business performance metrics.
-   Reviewing the presence and rating of the customers on various online platforms.
-   Comprehending what consumers want and what drives their decision making.
-   Conducting the SWOT analysis of the business to ensure what new possibilities are there in business.
-   Using the collected data (from customer data to competitive data) to enhance the business performance metrics of a company.
-   Escalating revenue growth with effective resource allocation skills
-   Understanding business needs and optimizing business operations accordingly.

Now that we understand that a **_BA roles and responsibilities_** in the two domains are completely different, it will be easier for us to understand the **_business analyst role description_** nuances in the analytics domain.

## Business Analyst Role Description

The **_BA roles and responsibilities_** in an organization mainly deal with devising unique business decisions that are practical and concrete. The following pointers explain the business analyst techniques gumption a candidate ought to have to justify this role:

### **Business Need Awareness**

The first and foremost skill demanded by a business analyst is sensing what the business needs to accomplish its auxiliary goals and finally hit the bull’s eye. To do so, SWOT Analysis is one technique that he should be familiar with. Mastering the nuances of the business’s strengths, opportunities, weaknesses, and threats will aid in making an informed decision and will give an organization the leverage of going toe-to-toe with its competitors. From marketing to sales to financial reports, SWOT analysis is the cog of any business to operate efficiently.

### **Historical and Current Business Data Evaluation**

What good does data bring if the analyst does not have the skill to evaluate it effectively? Well, **what does a Business Analyst do** in such a situation, here is the answer! After knowing what the business needs, a business analyst must put his critical thinking skills to some use. Investigation of data is powerful and understanding the analogy and psychology of it can lead to infinite possibilities of conscious decision making. 

For instance, in the early 1900s, Claude C Hopkins, an American executive was able to boost the Pepsodent toothpaste demand in just three weeks when an entourage of door-to-door people was already selling kinds of tubes of toothpaste to help American people with their dental problems and failing tremendously. He stated in his biography – _My Life in Advertising and Scientific Advertising_, “I made for myself a million dollars on Pepsodent.”

His critical thinking and problem-solving skills helped him understand the analogy of the right human psychology, and that is what a business needs as it is all about its target audience. A business Analyst has to compare the present and past data to derive an authentic conclusion.

### **Documentation of The Findings for A Concrete Business Understanding**

Business is all about the communication of ideas, solutions, and productivity in the form of numbers and figures. **An entry-level Business Analyst** will also know the paramount importance of documentation of the research and solutions related to business. Here is what graphical documentation looks like:

### **Examining and Enforcing the Expedient Solution**

Once a feasible solution is derived from all the possibilities available, it is time for a business analyst to allocate the resources within the business accordingly. This means he will update the performance matrix of the business and align it with the solution in a way that marketing operations perform to the best of their ability.

With the right solutions is it not just individual lives that can shift for better but, also companies, organizations, and communities. It is important to understand the dynamics of business when optimizing decisions, as nothing is permanent. This means **Business Analyst skills** need constant up-gradation which is congruent to an in-depth study of a particular industry – facts and figures.

## Business Analyst Salary

When talking about pay scale, let us be certain about one thing; there is no fixed pay scale for any job. Several factors such as your qualification, job experience, the type of company you are working for, and the location of your job, all play a significant role in determining the **_business analyst salary_**.

Since Business Analysis is a new field, the demand is certainly high, and so are the average salaries. Yet, to give a definite number is not feasible. But if one must, Rs. 55,000 per month is the average salary of a business analyst in India. You may say for a business analyst the salary ranges between $80,000 and $90,000 p.a. that is slightly higher than what a data analyst gets.  This figure is usually excluding all the perks, tips, and bonuses.

Structural changes and innovation administration are an integral part of **business analyst skills**. The insights an analyst provides play a crucial role in defining or reengineering the functions of an organization. Since the archaic period, critical thinking has been a point of discussion. The Greek philosophers Plato and Socrates have also provided the world with insightful books showing the foremost importance of critical thinking. To think logically about an idea or a situation is what is expected from a business analyst. As his one wrong move can cause the business huge loss in terms of money, performance, appearance, and goodwill. Among the very important business analyst job responsibilities is to examine critical thinking elements; hindsight, and foresight effectively.

### **Computer Skills**

As stated above, documentation of the findings is a fundamental skill. Therefore, to put the information in graphical and statistical form, acquiring computer knowledge is a must. Also, for an **entry-level business analyst** conducting research related to market, trends, consumer behavior, competitors techniques is a prerequisite. Therefore, understanding the basics of computers is mandatory to apply for this job. From Microsoft Word to PowerPoint to MS Excel a business analyst must know these tools through and through.

### **Analytics Skills**

This is the first skill that a **Business Analyst Role description** demands. There is a fine line difference between critical thinking skills and analytical skills. Critical thinking is based on logical reasoning and the latter decides the judgment founded on this logical reasoning element. These skills include:

-   Cost analysis
-   Predictive analysis
-   Industry analysis
-   Process analysis
-   Inventory analysis
-   Leadership analysis
-   Consumer behavior analysis
-   Risk analysis
